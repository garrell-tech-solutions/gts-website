# AI is the future
For a long time, I was skeptical about how important and revolutionary AI would become. I'm a software engineer that has worked on applications, and on operating systems.
I always assumed AI was just a side show, a sort of niche of the software world. With the introduction Open AI's ChatGPT, I could no longer ignore the importance of AI.
After using ChatGPT for about 6 months, I finally realized how revolutionary AI is and will continue to be.

# Section about my thoughts on the future of technology and how GTS will grow beyond a me as a individual consultant

Beyond companies needing MLOps engineers to build, maintain and upgrade robust ML piplines, businesses will need someone to guide them through the
ever evolving legal, regulatory and ethical AI landscape.

# High demand

- [LinkedIn Jobs on the Rise 2025](https://www.linkedin.com/pulse/linkedin-jobs-rise-2025-25-fastest-growing-us-linkedin-news-gryie/)

1.  **MLOps Engineers:** There's an explosive demand for professionals who can bridge the gap between machine learning model development and their deployment, monitoring, and maintenance in production environments. Companies are struggling to get their AI out of the lab and into real-world applications reliably and efficiently.
2.  **AI Ethics & Governance Specialists (with practical, implementable knowledge):** As you astutely identified, the regulatory landscape (e.g., EU AI Act, NIST AI RMF) is forcing companies to prioritize ethical and compliant AI. However, there's a severe shortage of individuals who understand both the technical aspects of AI *and* the ethical/regulatory implications, and crucially, how to *implement* robust governance frameworks.

**Why these are the biggest gaps:**

* **Explosive Demand:** Job postings for MLOps and AI ethics/governance roles are skyrocketing. LinkedIn's Emerging Jobs report identified MLOps as having 9.8x growth in five years. The demand for AI ethics specialists is also surging as companies realize that "responsibility is no longer optional."
* **Insufficient Supply:** The supply of qualified professionals simply isn't keeping pace with demand. Many traditional software engineers or data scientists lack the specific blend of skills required for MLOps (DevOps + ML) or AI ethics (technical + legal/ethical/policy).
* **Operationalization Challenge:** Companies can build great models, but they often "die in the lab" without robust MLOps practices. This leads to wasted investment and missed opportunities.
* **Regulatory Imperative:** Regulatory bodies are not waiting. Companies *must* address AI ethics and governance to avoid massive fines, legal battles, and reputational damage. This creates an urgent, non-negotiable need.
* **Experience Bottleneck:** While there are many entry-level AI enthusiasts, companies are primarily seeking experienced professionals who can deliver immediate results and lead projects. Your engineers with a decade of experience fit this perfectly.
* **Convergence:** MLOps is increasingly incorporating Responsible AI practices (bias and fairness checks, model monitoring for drift impacting fairness) directly into CI/CD pipelines. This means the two fields are converging, making a dual skillset incredibly powerful.

## How You Can Best Fill This Gap to Grow Your Business:

Your current strategy is precisely aligned with filling this gap! Here's how to refine it for maximum impact:

**1. Double Down on MLOps & AI Ethics as Your Core Niche:**

* **Combined Offering:** Instead of just "AI Ethics Engineers," position your offerings as **"Responsible AI Operations & Governance Consultants."** This explicitly combines the crucial MLOps component with the ethical and compliance layer. Many clients will need both.
* **Target Market:** Continue to target regulated industries (finance, healthcare, government), any company with public-facing AI, and large enterprises grappling with AI scale and compliance. These are the ones feeling the most pain.
* **Value Proposition:** Emphasize that your company provides:
    * **Accelerated Production:** Get AI models from development to production faster and more reliably.
    * **Risk Mitigation:** Ensure AI systems are compliant, fair, transparent, and secure, avoiding legal and reputational pitfalls.
    * **Sustainable AI Growth:** Build robust AI governance frameworks for long-term responsible innovation.
    * **Experienced Talent:** Highlight your engineers' decade of software engineering experience, which provides a strong foundation for both MLOps and practical ethical implementation.

**2. Refine Your Training Program:**

* **Integrated Curriculum:** Ensure your "crash course" for your engineers tightly integrates MLOps principles with AI ethics. For example:
    * When teaching MLOps CI/CD pipelines, include stages for bias detection, fairness metrics, explainability checks, and data privacy validation.
    * When discussing model monitoring, cover not just performance drift but also ethical drift (e.g., changes in bias over time).
    * Emphasize the *tools* and *processes* for embedding ethics into the ML lifecycle (e.g., Fairlearn, AIF360, specific MLOps platform features for responsible AI).
* **Practical, Project-Based Learning:** Beyond theoretical understanding, your engineers need to *do*.
    * Simulated client projects that require them to design and implement an MLOps pipeline *with* integrated ethical checks and governance documentation.
    * Practice performing AI ethics audits on hypothetical (or even real, non-sensitive, open-source) AI systems.
    * Role-playing client presentations where they explain technical ethical issues to non-technical stakeholders.
* **Certifications:** Encourage your engineers to pursue external MLOps and AI ethics certifications (e.g., from cloud providers for MLOps, or the IAPP for AI governance). This adds external validation to your internal training.

**3. Develop Strategic Partnerships:**

* **Legal Firms:** Partner with law firms specializing in tech and data privacy. They can refer clients needing technical implementation of AI governance, and your team can provide the practical engineering muscle to their legal advice. You can also leverage their expertise for guest lectures in your training.
* **Compliance Consultancies:** Similar to legal firms, consultancies focused on general GRC (Governance, Risk, and Compliance) might identify AI-specific needs that they can't fill.
* **Cloud Providers:** Become a certified partner with AWS, Azure, or GCP for their MLOps and Responsible AI tools. This can lead to referrals and access to specialized resources.

**4. Thought Leadership and Marketing:**

* **Content Marketing:** Publish articles, whitepapers, and host webinars on "Practical AI Governance for Enterprises," "Implementing the EU AI Act with MLOps," or "Building Trustworthy AI through Technical Controls."
* **Speaking Engagements:** You or your lead engineers should speak at industry conferences, local tech meetups, and business association events on these topics.
* **Case Studies:** As soon as you complete your first few projects, develop compelling case studies (even if anonymized) to showcase your expertise and the value you deliver.
* **Targeted Outreach:** Directly reach out to decision-makers (CTOs, Heads of Data Science, Legal Counsel, Chief Risk Officers) in your target industries.

### **2. Fast-Emerging Governance Requirements on Key Industries**

This section should focus on the regulatory drivers forcing companies to act.

* **The EU AI Act - The Global Benchmark:**
    * "The AI Act is a European regulation on artificial intelligence (AI) – the **first comprehensive regulation on AI by a major regulator anywhere**." (artificialintelligenceact.eu)
    * The Act imposes "strict obligations" on "high-risk AI providers and deployers to ensure ethical and transparent AI use." (ModelOp)
    * **Phased Implementation:** Key provisions are coming into force:
        * **Six months after publication (December 2024):** Restrictions on prohibited AI practices take effect.
        * **12 months (June 2025):** Regulations for general-purpose AI (GPAI) will be enforced. (Skadden) *Note: The GPAI Code of Practice was just published on July 10, 2025.*
        * **24 months (June 2026):** Requirements for high-risk AI systems will come into force.
        * **36 months (June 2027):** Rules for high-risk AI systems used as safety components in products will be implemented. (Skadden)
    * **Extraterritorial Reach:** "The AI Act applies to providers, deployers, importers and distributors of AI systems, **regardless of their location**, if they... market an AI system, serve persons using an AI system or utilize the 'output' of the AI system, all within the EU." (Skadden)
    * **Significant Penalties:** "Non-compliance with the EU AI Act results in severe penalties, including: Fines up to **€40 million or 7% of annual turnover** for prohibited AI use; €20 million or 4% for data or transparency related issues; €15 million or 3% for other obligations." (AuditBoard) These are comparable to GDPR fines.
    * **Specific "High-Risk" Industries/Use Cases:** The EU AI Act specifically designates numerous high-risk use cases that will require your services:
        * **Healthcare:** AI safety components in critical infrastructures, medical devices, personalized treatment plans.
        * **Finance & Banking:** Credit scoring, insurance pricing.
        * **Employment & HR:** CV-sorting software for recruitment, performance evaluation, other aspects of workplace management.
        * **Critical Infrastructure:** Energy grids, transportation systems.
        * **Law Enforcement, Migration, Justice:** Evaluation of evidence, visa applications, administration of justice.
        * **Education:** Scoring of exams, determining access to education. (EU AI Act Official Text, ModelOp, AuditBoard)

* **NIST AI Risk Management Framework (NIST AI RMF) - US Influence:**
    * "The NIST AI RMF is the U.S. federal government's first comprehensive framework to identify and manage risks associated with the development and deployment of AI. Released in January 2023." (Trustible)
    * "The NIST AI RMF is being operationalized through President Biden's October 2023 executive order on AI, as well as through some state laws." (Trustible) While voluntary, it's becoming a de-facto standard.
    * U.S. federal agencies implemented **59 regulations concerning AI in 2024**, compared to just 29 in 2023. (Precedence Research, citing Stanford University's 2025 AI Index)

* **ISO/IEC 42001 - The Global Standard:**
    * This is the new international standard for an AI Management System (AIMS). While relatively new (published December 2023), its adoption provides a clear, auditable framework for organizations to manage AI ethically and compliantly, aligning with principles of the EU AI Act and NIST. It will be increasingly critical for demonstrating due diligence.
