Here’s a **clear, categorized list of professions that already require licensure** (in the U.S., with notes where it’s broadly similar internationally). This should give you a solid baseline for comparison in your article.

---

## Healthcare & Medical Professions

Licensing here is justified by **direct risk to life and health**.

- Physicians (MD, DO)
- Surgeons
- Dentists
- Dental Hygienists
- Nurses (RN, LPN/LVN, NP)
- Physician Assistants (PA)
- Pharmacists
- Psychologists
- Psychiatrists
- Physical Therapists
- Occupational Therapists
- Speech-Language Pathologists
- Optometrists
- Chiropractors
- Veterinarians
- Paramedics / EMTs
- Radiologic Technologists
- Respiratory Therapists
- Midwives (licensed / certified)

---

## Legal & Judicial Professions

Licensing protects **rights, due process, and access to justice**.

- Attorneys / Lawyers
- Judges (typically must be licensed attorneys)
- Notaries Public (limited but licensed)
- Court Reporters (in many jurisdictions)
- Patent Attorneys / Agents

---

## Engineering & Technical Professions

Licensing is usually **contextual**—required when signing off on public safety–critical work.

- Professional Engineers (PE)
  - Civil Engineers
  - Structural Engineers
  - Mechanical Engineers
  - Electrical Engineers
  - Environmental Engineers

- Surveyors / Land Surveyors
- Architects
- Landscape Architects

> Notably: **Most engineers are not licensed**, but licensure is required when work impacts public safety or infrastructure.

---

## Construction & Skilled Trades

Licensing here focuses on **public safety, building integrity, and fire risk**.

- Electricians
- Plumbers
- HVAC Technicians
- General Contractors
- Building Inspectors
- Elevator Installers / Inspectors
- Fire Protection System Installers
- Asbestos Abatement Professionals

---

## Financial & Fiduciary Professions

Licensing exists due to **financial risk and asymmetric information**.

- Certified Public Accountants (CPA)
- Auditors
- Actuaries
- Investment Advisors (RIA)
- Stockbrokers (Series licenses)
- Insurance Agents / Brokers
- Mortgage Loan Originators
- Appraisers (real estate, business)

---

## Education & Child-Focused Professions

Licensing protects **minors and vulnerable populations**.

- Public School Teachers
- School Administrators
- Special Education Teachers
- School Counselors
- Childcare Providers (regulated at state level)

---

## Transportation & Heavy Equipment

Licensing ensures **public safety at scale**.

- Commercial Pilots
- Air Traffic Controllers
- Aircraft Mechanics
- Ship Captains / Maritime Officers
- Commercial Truck Drivers (CDL)
- Train Engineers
- Crane Operators

---

## Public Safety & Security

Licensing exists due to **use of force or public authority**.

- Police Officers
- Firefighters
- Security Guards (armed/unarmed)
- Private Investigators
- Bail Bondsmen
- Corrections Officers

---

## Real Estate & Property

Licensing protects against **fraud and misrepresentation**.

- Real Estate Agents
- Real Estate Brokers
- Property Managers
- Home Inspectors

---

## Personal Services (Often Overlooked)

These are interesting comparisons for software, because the **harm is usually limited**, yet licensure exists.

- Barbers
- Cosmetologists
- Estheticians
- Nail Technicians
- Massage Therapists
- Tattoo Artists / Body Piercers (often licensed or permitted)

> This category often comes up in licensing debates because the bar for entry can be higher than for software development.

---

## Key Pattern (Useful for Your Article)

Licensure usually appears when **one or more** of these are true:

1. **Risk of physical harm or death**
2. **Irreversible consequences**
3. **Large public impact**
4. **Information asymmetry** (clients can’t easily judge quality)
5. **Public trust or authority**
6. **Need for ethical enforcement**
7. **Difficulty for consumers to evaluate competence**

Software increasingly meets **several** of these criteria.
